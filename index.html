Who's In The System

8.3 billion people, three roles

Not everyone works the same way. The population splits into three groups with different relationships to the economy — paid labor, unpaid civic contribution, and unconditional support.

The Financing Model

Delper's infrastructure loan

Delper provides the upfront capital to build the automated production network and receives amortized repayment plus interest. Every worker's gross economic output has to cover both their own wage and a share of that debt service — the two numbers together are what "gross value per worker" actually means.

Half of what's freed continues to Delper as an ongoing royalty for providing the seed capital; the other half distributes to workers by tier, weighted to reward those who've upskilled furthest. Both sides then reinvest 50% of their share into a joint R&D fund — roughly $19T/year — targeting next-generation automation that eventually cuts the mandatory workweek toward 10 hours without reducing pay.

Coordination Layer

How it's run day to day

A decentralized, open-source platform coordinates the whole system — matching people to tasks, verifying work, and releasing payment. It functions as a shared utility, not a corporate product.

01 · Identity

Skills & Desires Registry

Privacy-first profiles using decentralized identifiers — people control their own data.

02 · Matching

Local Needs Matrix

Communities log needs directly; an algorithm matches by proximity and interest, not top-down assignment.

03 · Verification

Verification Consensus Protocol

Peer-to-peer check-ins confirm activity without invasive surveillance.

04 · Payout

Dividend Oracle

Once verified, wages and amenity credits release directly to the person's wallet.

Rollout

Ten-year transition

Years 1–3 · Foundation

Build while stabilizing

CapEx: $150T/yr, automation at 10%.

Activity: ~1B displaced workers shift into environmental cleanup and community care.

Goal: establish food, shelter, and medical security before asking people to change how they work.

Years 4–7 · Acceleration

Scale automation, upskill in parallel

CapEx: $65T/yr tapering, automation reaches 70%.

Activity: 3B people enroll in the upskilling pipeline — robotics maintenance, systems coordination, mediation.

Goal: move the workforce out of manual labor before the wage system is asked to fully replace it.

Years 8–10 · Steady State

Full self-sustenance

OpEx: $321T/yr, zero further CapEx.

Goal: government-owned factory revenue fully covers operations — wages, amenities, and Delper's debt service — without new capital injection.
